Egyptian Mythology

Egyptian mythology is a vast collection of stories about ancient Egyptian gods and goddesses, their interactions, and their roles in creating and maintaining the world. These myths are deeply intertwined with religious beliefs, rituals, and the daily lives of the ancient Egyptians. The mythology explains the creation of the world, the cycles of nature, the roles of different deities, and the journey of the soul after death.

Osiris

God of the afterlife, resurrection, and judge of the dead.

Isis

Goddess of magic, healing, and motherhood, known for reviving Osiris and protecting Horus.

Horus

Sky god and protector of the pharaoh, often depicted as a falcon.

Set

God of chaos, storms, and desert, who murdered his brother Osiris.

Ptah

Creator god and patron of craftsmen and architects.

Ra

Sun god who traveled across the sky by day and through the underworld by night.

Hathor

Goddess of love, beauty, music, and motherhood.

Anubis

God of mummification and guide of souls in the afterlife.

Thoth

God of writing, wisdom, and the moon, who kept records and maintained the universe's balance.

Bastet

Goddess of home, fertility, and protection, often depicted as a lioness or cat.

Amun

King of the gods and god of air and hidden things, later merged with Ra as Amun-Ra.
