Greek Mythology

Greek mythology is a collection of stories originating in ancient Greece, encompassing tales of gods, goddesses, heroes, heroines, and mythical creatures. These narratives, initially passed down orally, explain the world's origins, the nature of the divine, and the human condition. They were central to ancient Greek culture, shaping their religious beliefs, rituals, and understanding of morality.

Zeus

King of the gods, ruler of Mount Olympus, and god of the sky and thunder.

Hera

Queen of the gods, goddess of marriage and birth, and wife of Zeus.

Poseidon

God of the sea, earthquakes, and horses, brother of Zeus and Hades.

Demeter

Goddess of agriculture, harvest, and fertility, sister and consort of Zeus.

Athena

Goddess of wisdom, warfare, and crafts, daughter of Zeus.

Apollo

God of music, arts, knowledge, healing, plague, and prophecy, son of Zeus, brother of Artemis.

Artemis

Goddess of the hunt, wilderness, wild animals, the Moon, and archery, daughter of Zeus and sister of Apollo.

Ares

God of war, violence, and bloodshed, son of Zeus and Hera.

Hephaestus

God of fire, metalworking, crafts, and blacksmiths, son of Hera.

Aphrodite

Goddess of love, beauty, desire, and pleasure, daughter of Ouranous.

Hermes

Messenger of the gods, god of trade, thieves, travelers, sports, athletes, and border crossings, son of Zeus.

Hestia

Goddess of the hearth, home, and family, daughter of Kronos.

Dionysus

God of wine, celebrations, ecstasy, and theatre, son of Zeus.
