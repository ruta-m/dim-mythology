Indian Mythology

Indian mythology, primarily encompassing Hindu mythology, is a rich collection of narratives, beliefs, and cultural traditions originating from the Indian subcontinent. These myths, found in sacred texts like the Vedas, Puranas, and epics such as the Mahabharata and Ramayana, explain the world's creation, offer moral lessons, and explore the human condition. Key figures include deities like Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva, along with various other gods, goddesses, demons, and heroes, each with their own unique stories and attributes.

Ganesha

The elephant-headed god of wisdom, remover of obstacles, and patron of arts and intellect.

Shiva

The destroyer and transformer of the universe, often depicted in deep meditation or performing the cosmic dance.

Krishna

The god of compassion, love, and divine play, revered for his teachings in the Bhagavad Gita and his childhood exploits.

Rama

The ideal king, embodiment of virtue and dharma, and the hero of the epic Ramayana.

Hanuman

The devoted monkey god, symbol of strength, loyalty, and selfless service, and a key figure in the Ramayana.

Vishnu

The preserver of the universe, who incarnates in various forms (avatars) to restore cosmic order, including as Rama and Krishna.

Lakshmi

The goddess of wealth, prosperity, and fortune, often worshipped for material and spiritual abundance.

Durga

The fierce warrior goddess who protects the world from evil, embodying power and strength.

Kali

The goddess of destruction and transformation, representing the fierce aspect of divine energy.

Saraswati

The goddess of wisdom, knowledge, music, and arts, often depicted playing the veena.
