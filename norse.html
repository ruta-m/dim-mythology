Norse Mythology

Norse mythology encompasses the beliefs, stories, and religious practices of the North Germanic peoples, particularly during the Viking Age. It centers around a rich pantheon of gods and goddesses, mythical creatures, and epic tales of creation, destruction, and the cyclical nature of the universe.

Odin

The Allfather and chief of the gods, associated with wisdom, war, and death.

Frigg

Odin’s wife, goddess of marriage, motherhood, and foresight.

Thor

The god of thunder and storms, known for his strength and wielding the hammer Mjölnir.

Loki

A trickster god of mischief and deception, often causing chaos among the gods.

Freya

The goddess of love, beauty, fertility, and battle, and a member of the Vanir gods.

Baldr

The beloved god of light, purity, and innocence, whose death marks the beginning of Ragnarök.

Hel

The ruler of the underworld, who governs the realm where souls of the dead reside.

Tyr

The god of war and justice, known for sacrificing his hand to bind the wolf Fenrir.

Njörðr

The god of the sea, wind, and wealth, and father of Freyr and Freya.

Freyr

The god of fertility, prosperity, and peace, associated with growth and good harvests.

Heimdall

The watchman of the gods, known for his keen senses and guarding the Bifröst bridge.

Hermóðr

The brave son of Odin, who rode to Hel’s realm to rescue Balder’s soul after his death.
